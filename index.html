Document ID: Site-02-STAFF-GUID-122925
Security Clearance Required: Level 2 (RESTRICTED)
Authorized Personnel Only: All Site-02 Staff Members
Issued By: O5-1 (via Site-02 Command Authority)
Effective Date: 30 December 2025

WARNING: Unauthorized access, reproduction, or dissemination of this document constitutes a severe breach of Foundation security protocols and will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and amnestic administration.
Subject: Staff Conduct, Administrative Protocol, and Disciplinary Guidelines for Site-02 Personnel

To all staff members assigned to Site-02,

These guidelines establish the mandatory standards of conduct, administrative responsibilities, and disciplinary procedures for all Foundation personnel at Site-02. As stewards of containment and security, staff are expected to uphold the highest levels of professionalism, impartiality, and dedication.

Compliance is mandatory. Violations will be reviewed by Upper Administration (UA) and may result in formal reprimand, suspension, demotion, removal from staff duties, or termination. All infractions will incur a strike on your personnel record, with strikes expiring after one month unless appealed to UA.

1. General Staff Rules
1. All staff must adhere strictly to server rules at all times.
2. Maintain consistent activity on the server to ensure operational readiness.
3. For absences exceeding three days, submit a formal Leave of Absence (LOA) request to a UA member in advance.
4. Respond promptly to all staff requests or summons unless prevented by extenuating circumstances.
5. Repeated failure to respond to requests under Rule 4 will result in disciplinary measures.
6. Staff are strictly prohibited from accessing the server while under the influence of any impairing substance or condition that may compromise judgment.
7. Administrative duties take precedence over roleplay. Staff must respond to all !mod requests unless another staff member is actively handling them.
8. All administrative sits must be conducted in the designated moderation room.
9. Teleportation (TP) is permitted only with a valid operational reason; unauthorized use will result in punishment.
10. Bans must be issued using the exact command format: :ban [name] [reason].
11. Staff are prohibited from using administrative powers against other staff members, except UA personnel.
12. Administrative powers must not be used during roleplay scenarios.
13. Staff may host minor events only with prior approval from the Roleplay Manager or Server Host.
14. Maintain full composure in all interactions, regardless of provocation.
15. Staff must have recording software active and running at all times while on the server to facilitate accurate incident documentation and prevent false reports.
16. Act with maturity at all times, including on Discord servers, direct messages, and forums associated with Site-02.
17. Witnesses in investigations must be limited to Builders or Staff; unauthorized witnesses are prohibited.
18. [Priority] Staff must be absolutely certain a rule violation has occurred before taking action.
19. Exercise common sense when rules are ambiguous; higher-ranking staff decisions supersede lower ranks.
20. Bias in any form is strictly prohibited.
21. Demeaning or derogatory language toward players is forbidden; friendly interactions are encouraged.
22. Staff must have immediate access to current rules while online, as they are subject to frequent updates.
23. [Priority] Maintain active presence on the server, Discord, and forums.
24. Lying to players is prohibited unless explicitly authorized by UA.
25. Staff must recite current rules to players upon request, given frequent changes.
26. Observing other staff members' sits is forbidden without UA permission.
27. Report staff issues or server problems to UA immediately; escalate to Brookline91 if UA cannot resolve.
28. All Level 1 and Level 2 offenses require an administrative sit; Level 3 offenses do not.
29. Do not remain AFK on Staff on Duty (SOD) for more than three minutes; extensions will result in a warning.
30. When issuing bans, include the exact ban reason in the warning message.

2. Ban and Offense Classification
Offenses are categorized by severity level. Punishments are at staff discretion based on repetition and context.

2.1 Level 1 Offenses (Minimal impact on roleplay and operations):
• Random Killing (RK)
• Fail Roleplay (FailRP)
• Job Abuse (e.g., breaching SCPs as Foundation staff)
• Breaking New Life Rule (NLR)
• Minor Disrespect
• Interrupting Administrative Sits
• Chat Spamming
• Lying to Staff
• Leaving to Avoid Roleplay (LTARP)
• Calling !mod without valid reason
• Any other minor infractions

Punishment: Verbal/written warning, kick, or 2-hour ban (escalates with repetition/severity).

2.2 Level 2 Offenses (Moderate impact, semi-halting roleplay and operations):
• Mass Random Killing (3+ kills)
• Repetitive FailRP
• Repetitive NLR Violations
• Racism or Minor Sexism
• Multiple Level 1 Offenses
• Major Disrespect
• Disobeying Staff Directives
• Minor Exploits (e.g., map glitching)
• Threatening Players
• Purposeful False Accusations
• Leaving to Avoid Punishment (LTAP; adds 2 days to punishment)

Punishment: 1-day to 1-week ban, documented with warning. Repeat offenders: 1-week to 3-week ban.

2.3 Level 3 Offenses (Major impact, threatening server integrity):
• DDoS Threats (Permanent)
• Major Exploits
• Hacking
• Multiple Level 2 Offenses
• Doxing (as determined by UA)

Punishment: 3-week to permanent ban.

2.4 Level 4 Offenses (Critical breaches handled exclusively by UA):
• Multiple Level 3 Offenses
• Mass Harassment
• Actual DDoS
• UA Abuse of Power
• Extortion

Punishment: Determined by Upper Administration.

3. Strike System for Staff Misconduct
Strikes track staff infractions and determine ongoing eligibility.

• Strike 1: Warning for large mistakes.
• Strike 2: Demotion or equivalent punishment.
• Strike 3: Removal from the staff team.

Strikes expire after one month or upon successful appeal to UA.

These guidelines are effective immediately and supersede all prior protocols. Questions or appeals must be directed to UA.

Secure. Contain. Protect.

Signed,
O5-1

Subject: Operational and Roleplay Guidelines for SCP Entity Actors at Site-02

To all personnel authorized to roleplay SCP anomalous entities,

The following guidelines establish the mandatory standards for portraying SCP entities within Site-02. These rules ensure consistent, fair, and lore-appropriate roleplay while maintaining operational security and containment integrity. They apply to all SCP actors, regardless of specific entity designation.

Violations will result in administrative review, warnings, temporary role restrictions, or permanent removal from SCP roleplay privileges.

1. Weaponization of Anomalous Entities
1.1 Foundation personnel are strictly prohibited from weaponizing any SCP entity.
1.2 Chaos Insurgency personnel may weaponize designated SCPs only as explicitly listed in their respective entity guidelines. Very limited exceptions may be granted on a case-by-case basis by Site Command.

2. SCP Presence in D-Block (CDC)
2.1 Passive and approved item-based SCPs are permitted within D-Block.
2.2 Each SCP entity's individual guidelines specify whether entry into D-Block is authorized.
2.3 SCPs requiring D-Block access must receive explicit authorization from Foundation personnel; self-authorization is prohibited.

3. Item-Based Anomalies
3.1 Actors portraying item-based SCPs may carry only one (1) anomalous item at any time.

4. Detainment of SCP Entities
4.1 SCP entities may not be detained unless explicitly required by their designated containment procedures.

5. Detain-Based Dragging
5.1 SCP entities are prohibited from dragging players while under detain mechanics. Exceptions will be clearly listed in individual entity guidelines.

6. Silent Breaches
6.1 Silent breaches may only be initiated by other SCP entities or Chaos Insurgency Scouts.

7. Communication Privileges
7.1 Humanoid SCPs capable of speech may utilize in-game communication devices if provided.
7.2 Use of external Discord or out-of-character communication channels to relay in-game information is strictly prohibited (SCP-953 is explicitly excepted from this restriction).

8. Improper Containment Procedures
8.1 Deliberate or negligent improper containment (e.g., leaving containment chambers open or failing to activate required mechanisms such as the Femur Breaker for SCP-106) permits the affected SCP to re-breach after a 15-second grace period.

9. Antagonization During Recontainment
9.1 SCP entities that are intentionally antagonized during active recontainment may engage the provoking individual in self-defense before voluntarily returning to containment.

10. Access to Player Spawn Areas
10.1 SCP entities are prohibited from entering designated player bunk or spawn areas.
10.2 Players may not exploit this restriction by fleeing to spawn areas to evade pursuing SCP entities.

11. Breach Prerequisites
11.1 SCP entities may not initiate a breach unless at least five (5) Foundation personnel are actively online within the facility.

12. Interaction with Chaos Insurgency Base and Metagaming
12.1 SCP entities are prohibited from entering or approaching the Chaos Insurgency base under any circumstances.
12.2 Metagaming—defined as the use of out-of-character knowledge to gain an in-character advantage—is forbidden. This includes, but is not limited to: spawncamping, retaining memory across deaths (violating New Life Rule), or locating hidden bases without logical in-character justification derived from lore, prior roleplay events, or entity-specific abilities.

13. Chain Breaching by SCP Entities
13.1 An SCP entity breached by another SCP may not subsequently breach additional entities.
13.2 Explicit exception: SCP-035, which requires SCP-049 to initiate its breach.

14. Teaming with Class-D Personnel
14.1 Certain SCP entities are prohibited from cooperating with Class-D personnel. Allowed collaborations will be clearly specified in the respective entity's individual guidelines.

15. Weapon Restrictions
15.1 Certain SCP entities are prohibited from possessing or utilizing specific weaponry (e.g., FrostTech devices, SPAS-12).
15.2 Exceptions are granted only to entities with explicit requirements, such as SCP-682 or those necessitating mesh-related containment overrides.

Adherence to these guidelines is essential for maintaining immersion, fairness, and the integrity of Site-02 operations.

Secure. Contain. Protect.

Signed,
O5-1
Overseer Council Head

Subject: Operational Conduct and Discipline Guidelines for Level 3 Clearance Mobile Task Force Personnel at Site-02

To all Mobile Task Force operatives holding Level 3 clearance,

The following guidelines outline the mandatory standards of operational discipline, tactical protocol, and professional conduct expected of all Mobile Task Force personnel assigned to Site-02. These rules apply universally to all units (including Nu-7, Epsilon-11, Tau-5, and attached detachments).

Compliance is mandatory. Violations will be reviewed by Site Command and may result in formal reprimand, suspension from active duty, demotion, or permanent removal from Mobile Task Force assignment.

1. Firearms Discipline
1.1 Random or unauthorized discharge of weapons is strictly prohibited. Firearms may only be discharged in response to confirmed hostile threats, active containment breaches, or under direct orders from commanding personnel.
1.2 Reckless firing, "spray and pray" tactics, or discharging weapons in non-combat areas will result in immediate disciplinary action.

2. Tactical Movement and Team Integrity
2.1 MTF operatives must operate in squads of at least two personnel at all times during active patrols, responses, or containment operations within the facility.
2.2 Lone-wolf actions are forbidden except in extreme circumstances where separation is unavoidable. Maintain visual or radio contact with squad members.
2.3 Coordinate movements and maintain proper spacing and formation to maximize operational effectiveness and minimize risk.

3. Communication and Professional Demeanor
3.1 All communications, both in voice and text channels, must remain respectful, official, and mission-focused while on duty.
3.2 Use clear, professional language. Avoid slang, memes, excessive abbreviations, or casual tone during operations.
3.3 Refrain from "goofing around," making jokes at inappropriate times, using emotes, or engaging in non-professional behavior that undermines unit discipline or site security.

4. Chain of Command and Obedience
4.1 Obey all lawful orders from higher-ranking personnel (including MTF officers, Level 4+ staff, and O5 Council members) promptly and without argument.
4.2 Respect the authority of Site Command, Scientific Department leads during joint operations, and Security Department personnel in non-breach scenarios.
4.3 Disputes or concerns regarding orders are to be raised through proper channels after the conclusion of active operations—never during a live incident.

5. General Operational Conduct
5.1 Maintain constant situational awareness and readiness.
5.2 Do not interfere with ongoing Scientific Department testing or experiments unless explicitly ordered or in direct response to a containment breach.
5.3 Treat all Foundation personnel (including D-Class under supervision) with professionalism. Harassment, intimidation, or unnecessary aggression is prohibited.
5.4 Post-incident, all operatives are required to submit accurate and timely reports on engagements and actions taken.

These protocols are designed to ensure the highest standards of discipline, coordination, and effectiveness in protecting Foundation assets and containing anomalous threats.

Secure. Contain. Protect.

O5-1

Subject: Standard Operating Procedures for Foundation Enforcement Division at Site-02

To all members of the Foundation Enforcement Division,

Foundation Enforcement serves as the primary internal security and penal enforcement arm of Site-02. Our mandate is to maintain order, enforce the Site Penal Code through lawful arrests, conduct investigations into corruption, and ensure the safe and efficient operation of the facility.

1. General Duties
1. Enforce the Site-02 Penal Code through the execution of lawful arrests.
2. Conduct regular patrols of Light Containment Zone (LCZ) and Heavy Containment Zone (HCZ).
3. Establish and maintain checkpoints throughout the facility as required.
4. Provide temporary guard coverage for Class-D Cell Block (CDC) when Security Department personnel are unavailable.
5. Escort and protect members of Site Command (SiC) or Ethics Committee (EC) upon request.
6. Assist Scientific Department personnel during testing and enforce the Foundation Code of Ethics when necessary.
7. Provide secondary support for SCP recontainment or maintenance operations (lowest priority).

2. Rules and Guidelines
1. Obey all lawful orders from superior officers within the chain of command. Concerns regarding orders must be raised privately to a Commissioned Officer or higher.
2. During Code Red or higher alert statuses, suspend all arrest activities. Prioritize SCP recontainment and CDC security. Crimes witnessed during elevated codes must be documented via warrant for post-incident resolution.
3. Maintain consistent activity. Initial inactivity will result in a formal warning; continued inactivity will lead to removal from the division.
4. Conduct all operations in a manner that upholds the professional reputation of Foundation Enforcement. Actions that damage the division's integrity will not be tolerated.

3. Strike System
• 1 Strike: Formal Warning
• 3 Strikes: Demotion
• 5 Strikes: Removal from Division
• 6+ Strikes: Blacklisting (duration determined by Command)

Strikes may be issued for:
1. Disobeying direct orders from Non-Commissioned Officers or higher while adhering to chain of command.
2. Conducting false or unlawful arrests.
3. Abusing disguise kits or other specialized equipment.
4. Being arrested as an FE member (subject to heightened scrutiny).
5. Actions that bring disrepute to Foundation Enforcement.

4. Important Operational Reminders
• Do not detain arrestees longer than necessary.
• All arrests must be logged and published in designated Foundation Enforcement channels.
• Penal Code jail times represent maximum sentences; reductions are permitted based on circumstances and severity.
• Maximum detention duration per arrest: 30 minutes.
• Hostage negotiation ransom guidelines:
• 5,000 credits: Non-Commissioned Officer
• 10,000 credits: High Command
• 15,000 credits: Site Command / Level 5 / Ethics Committee
• 20,000 credits: Site Director / Deputy Director
• 25,000 credits: O5 Council Member

5. Arrest Procedures
1. Remain calm and respectful toward the suspect at all times. Aggression may escalate the situation unnecessarily.
2. Utilize handcuffs as primary tool for swift and efficient detainment.
3. Exercise caution—non-compliant suspects or third-party interference may pose threats. Lethal force is authorized only when life is in imminent danger.
4. Apply reasonable and proportionate charges and sentencing based on crime severity.
5. Log and publish all arrests in official channels for supervisory review and coordination.

6. Patrol Routes
• Class-D Cell Block (CDC): Provide coverage upon Security request; operate with at least one additional FE member.
• Light Containment Zone (LCZ): Patrol the main loop, including R-Wing, all personnel wing containment chambers, SCP-662, SCP-049, SCP-173, Armory security check, and O-Wing status verification.
• Heavy Containment Zone (HCZ): Patrol Euclid Wing to endpoint, return via Arena-2 into Keter Zone, check all chambers to SCP-106, then return via Arena-1/3.
• Entrance Zone (EZ): Patrol only under direct Commissioned Officer supervision. Unauthorized presence may result in trespassing charges.

7. Ranking Structure
High Command
• Grand Marshal: Division Leader (full promotion/demotion authority)
• Field Marshal: Deputy Leader (promotion/demotion authority up to Captain)

Commissioned Officers
• Marshal: Third-in-command; oversees all operations and personnel
• Captain: Supervises NCOs (Enforcement Captain, Intelligence Captain; promotion authority up to Master Sergeant)

Non-Commissioned Officers
• Master Sergeant: Senior NCO (promotion/demotion up to Corporal)
• Staff Sergeant: (promotion/demotion up to Senior Officer)
• Sergeant: Entry-level NCO; conducts simulations and tryouts (promotion/demotion up to Officer)

Enlisted
• Corporal: Leadership candidate; conducts tryouts and limited strikes
• Senior Officer: Second specialization; evaluated for promotion potential
• Officer First Class: Increased operational freedom
• Officer: First specialization
• Auxiliary Officer: Entry-level; expected to demonstrate maturity and respect

8. Specialized Units
• Foundation Enforcement Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT): Elite operators specializing in hostage rescue/negotiation, high-command protection (when Alpha-1/Omega-1 unavailable), and execution of warrants/raids. Selection via tryout.
• Intelligence Agency: Investigative branch responsible for corruption probes, evidence gathering, and support for prosecutions. Selection via application reviewed by IA Lead, Senior Marshal, or Grand Marshal.

Secure. Contain. Protect.

Subject: Overview of the Research Department at Site-02

To all Level 3 and higher personnel,

The following memorandum provides a general overview of the Research Department at Site-02. This information is classified Level 3 and is intended to familiarize relevant personnel with the department's structure, responsibilities, and operational role within the Foundation.

1. Department Mission
The Research Department is responsible for the systematic study of anomalous objects, entities, and phenomena designated as SCPs. Its primary objective is to expand Foundation knowledge of anomalous properties, refine containment procedures, develop potential safe applications (where authorized), and mitigate risks associated with SCP exposure. All research activities are conducted with the core mandate: Secure, Contain, Protect.

2. Core Responsibilities
Design, execute, and document controlled experiments involving SCP entities and Class-D personnel.
Observe, record, and analyze the effects, behaviors, and interactions of anomalous objects under varying conditions.
Author and maintain accurate, detailed research logs, test reports, and addenda for inclusion in official SCP documentation.
Collaborate with Containment Specialists to improve and update containment protocols based on experimental findings.
Advise Site Command and the Ethics Committee on risks, potential cross-testing implications, and anomalous threat assessments.
Ensure all testing adheres strictly to the Foundation Code of Ethics and approved procedural guidelines.

3. Operational Scope
Research Department personnel operate primarily within the Light Containment Zone (LCZ), Heavy Containment Zone (HCZ), and designated testing chambers. Access to Entrance Zone (EZ) facilities is granted on a need-basis with appropriate escort. Testing typically involves:
Direct interaction between Class-D subjects and SCP objects.
Remote observation and instrumentation where direct exposure is deemed excessively hazardous.
Cross-testing between compatible SCP entities only with prior Level 4+ authorization and Ethics Committee review.
All test results must be logged promptly in the Site-02 research database for peer review and archival purposes.

4. Personnel Structure (General)
Chief of Research / Senior Researchers: Level 4 personnel responsible for department oversight, test approval, and high-risk experiment supervision.
Researchers: Primary Level 3 staff who propose, conduct, and document standard testing procedures.
Junior Researchers: Entry-level personnel under mentorship, assisting in observation, data collection, and basic testing support.
Containment Specialists: Embedded technical staff focused on chamber maintenance and procedural safety.

5. Key Operational Principles
All testing requires formal proposal submission and approval from a Senior Researcher or higher.
Class-D personnel are expendable assets allocated specifically for high-risk exposure testing.
Researchers must prioritize personnel safety, containment integrity, and ethical compliance above experimental curiosity.
Unauthorized testing, improvisation during active experiments, or deviation from approved protocols is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate suspension of research privileges.

The Research Department plays a critical role in advancing Foundation understanding of the anomalous while upholding the highest standards of security and ethical conduct.

Secure. Contain. Protect.

Document ID: Site-02-MEDICAL-OVERVIEW-L2
Security Clearance Required: Level 2 (RESTRICTED)
Authorized Personnel Only: Medical Department Staff and Relevant Personnel
Issued By: Chief Medical Officer
Effective Date: 30 December 2025

1. Department Mission
The Medical Department is responsible for the preservation of human life, treatment of injuries and illnesses, and maintenance of physical and psychological health for all Foundation personnel. In the context of anomalous phenomena, the department also conducts specialized medical evaluations, containment of biohazardous SCPs, and research into anomalous medical conditions and effects.

2. Core Responsibilities
Provide emergency medical care and trauma treatment during containment breaches, accidents, and combat incidents.
Perform routine medical examinations, vaccinations, and health monitoring for all site personnel.
Treat injuries and illnesses affecting Foundation staff, Mobile Task Force operatives, and Class-D personnel.
Manage the Site Infirmary and Medical Wing, including surgical suites, quarantine chambers, and pharmacy operations.
Conduct post-exposure evaluations and administer amnestics when required.
Monitor and treat anomalous medical conditions (e.g., cognitohazards, memetic infections, or SCP-induced physiological changes).
Collaborate with the Research Department on medical aspects of SCP testing and the Ethics Committee on humane treatment protocols.
Maintain medical records, incident reports, and autopsy documentation for all personnel fatalities.

3. Operational Scope
Medical Department personnel are stationed primarily in the Medical Wing and maintain teams deployable to any area of the facility. Key operational areas include:
Emergency response to containment breaches and site-wide incidents.
On-site surgical and critical care facilities.
Quarantine and isolation chambers for biohazardous or contagious anomalies.
Mobile medical teams accompanying high-risk operations (e.g., MTF deployments or Class-D escort).

4. Personnel Structure (General)
Chief Medical Officer / Senior Physicians: Level 4 personnel responsible for department oversight, policy development, and high-risk medical decisions.
Physicians / Surgeons: Level 3 staff who perform diagnoses, treatments, and surgical procedures.
Paramedics / Field Medics: Level 3 personnel trained for rapid trauma care and emergency response in the field.
Nurses / Medical Technicians: Support staff responsible for patient care, monitoring, and equipment maintenance.
Psychiatrists / Psychologists: Specialized personnel handling mental health, cognitohazard exposure, and psychological evaluations.

5. Key Operational Principles
All medical interventions must prioritize patient survival and containment integrity.
Treatment of Foundation personnel takes precedence over Class-D subjects unless otherwise directed by Site Command or the Ethics Committee.
Medical personnel are authorized to use lethal force only in defense of themselves or others when no other option exists.
Unauthorized use of medical resources, experimentation without approval, or deviation from established protocols is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate suspension of medical privileges.

The Medical Department is a critical pillar of Site-02, ensuring that personnel remain capable of fulfilling their duties in the face of both conventional and anomalous threats.

Document ID: Site-02-MTF-OVERVIEW-L3
Security Clearance Required: Level 3 (CONFIDENTIAL)
Authorized Personnel Only: MTF Personnel and Relevant Oversight
Issued By: MTF Regional Command
Effective Date: 30 December 2025

1. Mission Overview
Mobile Task Forces are specialized, highly trained units composed of the Foundation’s most capable operatives. They represent the primary rapid-response and tactical arm for handling containment breaches, hostile Group of Interest incursions, high-threat anomalous events, and specialized recontainment operations. MTFs are deployed when standard Security Department forces are insufficient to address the threat level.
Site-02 hosts or supports multiple MTF designations, including (but not limited to) Nu-7 "Hammer Down," Epsilon-11 "Nine-Tailed Fox," and other task forces as required by regional command.

2. Core Responsibilities
Rapid response to major containment breaches and facility-wide alerts.
Recontainment and neutralization of escaped SCP entities.
